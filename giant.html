Hiking

8/1/2022

Today, I embarked on a challenging hiking adventure with my friends, and I must admit, the trail exceeded all expectations. Initial online reviews that labeled it as difficult turned out to be a strikingly accurate assessment, debunking my initial assumption of exaggeration. The trail's predominant features seemed to be rugged rocks, and the scorching heat only added to the challenge.

One of my friends, a seasoned hiker, effortlessly sprinted ahead, leaving my other friend and me to navigate the demanding terrain at our own pace. The trail gradually grew more manageable with time, and the breathtaking view that awaited us at the summit justified every drop of sweat shed. When we finally took a well-deserved break, I found myself drenched and was grateful for the refreshing respite. We thought we would be able to finish the hike in 4 hours as the duration written on the website, but at the halfway point, it had taken us 3 hours instead of 2 due to how unprepared we were.

During our interlude, we enjoyed Pocky and madeleines, savoring the delightful contrast between the treats and the demanding hike. As we sat looking below at the water, we contemplated our summer. We were all stressed about college apps and pondered how we would feel a year from today. We discussed our struggles with the SAT and how hard it was to write our personal essays. My friend who was going to London explained the differences in her admissions process, which I found interesting as their process was quite different from the CommonApp.

The hike was hard, but in the end, I felt a sense of accomplishment from pulling myself through and was happy to spend the day with my friends! There was also the pleasant surprise of not having any mosquito bites, which I was glad for due to my mosquito allergy.
