New cuisine?

11/30/2022

Following our previous adventure, my friend and I eagerly returned to the city, our spirits high. Our mission this time was to explore the charming streets of Soho and savor the delectable offerings from various bakeries.

Our first stop was the renowned Dominique Ansel Bakery, where we embarked on a sweet journey with their legendary cronut. The experience was nothing short of heavenly, leaving us with a taste of indulgence that lingered long after the last bite.

Our quest for culinary delights then led us to Harbs, where I discovered the sheer brilliance of their matcha cake. Its delicate balance of flavors, not overly sweet, left a lasting impression. The crepe cake, an artistic marvel, further enriched our experience. To complement this delightful indulgence, we indulged in their royal milk tea, a choice I'd heartily recommend.

With our appetites satisfied, we ventured into a culinary fusion experience at LLama San, a Peruvian and Japanese fusion restaurant. As I was relatively unfamiliar with Peruvian cuisine, I approached this dining experience with eager anticipation. It was a welcome respite from a week marred by exams and assignments, where the thought of exploring new flavors acted as a motivating force. The prospect of trying the nigiri, which I had read about in the The New York Times, was particularly exciting.

The restaurant's ambiance was a thing of beauty, enveloped in soft lighting that added to the enchanting atmosphere. Surprisingly, the timing of our visit meant fewer patrons, an unexpected delight that allowed for more intimate and serene conversations with my friend. As we savored our meals, our conversation meandered through the intricacies of our lives, creating a memorable juxtaposition with the unique and delightful flavors that graced our palates.

Among the dishes, I found myself especially delighted by the noodle creation, a culinary masterpiece that left me thoroughly content and looking forward to future gastronomic adventures.
