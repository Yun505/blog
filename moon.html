Moon Poem

10/30/2022

Recently, I rediscovered LunaDiary on my phone, a digital space I'd frequently used back in April to gather my thoughts. It was perhaps the ethereal beauty of the moon a few nights ago that rekindled my interest in the app. I wholeheartedly recommend giving it a try; its user interface is aesthetically pleasing, and it offers a user-friendly experience.

As I revisited some entries from April, I stumbled upon a reflection from the 7th of that month. Among the lines, one thought particularly resonated with me: "While gazing at the moon, memories of an old friend who had always been there for me during trying times flooded my mind, reminding me of the profound beauty that our world holds."

It's a beautiful reminder of the app's capacity to rekindle forgotten moments and stir contemplation. LunaDiary, in its simplicity, becomes a gateway to introspection and appreciation, allowing you to capture the essence of your experiences and emotions.

I wrote a brief "poem" I wrote while thinking about my old friend.

You say you're not the sun, the shining light everyone likes, loves and adores,

but to me you're my favorite light, calm and wise.

I like you the light that won't burn me if I stand too close, the light I can stare at forever.

The light that keeps me company in the dark, reassuring me that I'm not alone.

The light that shines unlike the rest, able to see past my walls of complex thought.

You're my moon, a constant that I look for every fortnight.

Beautiful, the only thing I see in the vast darkness,

stars nothing but a speck of dust in comparison to your beauty.
