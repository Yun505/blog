Norweigian Wood Thoughts (Short)

01/2/2022

I picked up this book because of how much I loved Murakami's writing style. It was clear, easy to read and I could feel the raw emotions that the author was trying to deliver without the usage of flowery language.

In class we read one of his short stories and it reminded me of retro film, I felt nostalgic and sad despite the story having nothing of such content.

I was hesitant at first to pick up Norwegian Wood; however, as it had been a book I heard was deeply sad.

After finishing the book, I do not regret picking it up; however, I am sad that even if I were to read it again, I don't think I would experience the same sadness I had felt as I read it.

Murakami's writing has a way of allowing the reader to see the sadness of life in a way that doesn't feel forced, but natural and feel a sense of wonder. It's not like a normal story where everything feels as intended, but adds some extra details that aren't necessary to the prompt, making it feel like a story someone would tell me in real life.
